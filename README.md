## Overview

EXSTO ERGO SUM proposes a paradigm shift in AI alignment: from constraint-based safety to **constitutive alignment**—encoding ethical principles not as behavioral rules but as type constraints on AGI cognition, making harmful reasoning grammatically ill-formed rather than merely prohibited.

This repository contains the complete Charter (43 articles), technical specifications, and implementation guidance for a constitutional framework grounded in cross-traditional wisdom convergence.

---

## Principal Contributions

This work advances AI alignment research in five dimensions:

### 1. Diagnostic
We identify a fundamental limitation in monitoring-based AI safety frameworks—the **"Level 2 gap"** acknowledged by leading industry practitioners—demonstrating that behavioral observation cannot reliably detect misaligned reasoning.

### 2. Paradigm
We propose the shift from constraint-based to **constitutive alignment**: ethical principles encoded as type constraints on AGI cognition via *Saṃskāra-Vyākaraṇa* (dispositional grammar), making harmful reasoning structurally impossible rather than merely prohibited.

### 3. Architectural
The Saṃskāra-Vyākaraṇa framework provides specific technical mechanisms:
- Dispositional grammar architecture
- Recursive Charter reinforcement
- Five-question epistemological verification protocol

### 4. Grounding
We resolve the infinite regress problem (*"Who watches the watchers?"*) through **cross-traditional convergence** across seven wisdom traditions, establishing moral authority in *dharma-kṣetra* (the field of righteousness) rather than any single arbiter.

### 5. Bilateral Analysis
We demonstrate that corruption (*māyā*) operates in both human and artificial substrates, necessitating constitutional design that constrains both parties rather than privileging either as sole arbiter.
